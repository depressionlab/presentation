### 🎙️ Speaker Notes (50-Minute Script)

Print these notes out or keep them on a tablet next to you while presenting. The pacing is designed to leave roughly 10 minutes at the end for Q&A.

**Slide 1: Title (0:00 - 3:00)**
*   **Intro:** Welcome the audience. Explain that cybersecurity often feels like it's only for coders in hoodies. Today, we're tearing down that myth. 
*   **Hook:** "Today isn't about avoiding a virus; it's about reclaiming your digital autonomy from companies whose entire business model relies on turning your life into data."

**Slide 2: Live Demos (3:00 - 10:00)**
*   *Note: Spend a good chunk of time here. It sets the stakes.*
*   **Phishing Tab:** Show the email. Point out the specific details (sister's name, previous address). *Click the link, type a fake password, hit enter.* "This is what happens when public data gets weaponized. They didn't hack you; they manipulated you using data they bought."
*   **Data Broker Tab:** "Where did they get that data? Here." *Click the pulsing red blocks.* Explain that anyone can search this right now for free.
*   **Ads Tab:** *Click a few browsing items.* Point out how quickly the system categorizes you with labels like "Financial Distress."
*   **Fingerprint Tab:** *Click scan.* Explain that clearing cookies doesn't protect you if your browser configuration itself acts as a permanent nametag.

**Slide 3: Obligatory Book Tie-In (10:00 - 12:00)**
*   Connect James Vincent's *Beyond Measure* to the digital economy. 
*   "Humans measure what they want to control. Right now, tech companies are measuring *you*."

**Slide 4: The Philosophy (12:00 - 14:00)**
*   "If the product is free, you are the product."
*   Introduce **Data Minimization**: The golden rule of privacy. Does a Sudoku app need your location? If yes, it's spyware.

**Slide 5: "I have nothing to hide" (14:00 - 17:00)**
*   *This is your mic-drop moment.*
*   Read the bathroom analogy. Watch the audience's reaction; it usually clicks instantly.
*   "Privacy is not about secrecy. It's about dignity."

**Slide 6: Defining Terms (17:00 - 19:00)**
*   Briefly explain the difference between Privacy (who sees it), Security (is the vault locked), and Anonymity (wearing a mask).
*   Right now, users have none of these by default.

**Slide 7 & 8: Public Records & Brokers (19:00 - 23:00)**
*   Remind them of the demo. This data isn't stolen; it's scraped from DMV records, marriage licenses, and voter registries.
*   The system was built before the internet. When court records were physical paper, you had "practical obscurity" (someone had to drive to the courthouse). Now, it's indexed and searchable by a stalker halfway across the world in milliseconds.

**Slide 9: Why is this legal? (23:00 - 26:00)**
*   Compare US vs EU. 
*   "The US operates on an 'opt-out' model—companies can do whatever they want until you find the hidden button to stop them. The EU (GDPR) uses an 'opt-in' model."
*   Mention that massive lobbying money is spent keeping the US model unchanged.

**Slide 10: Real Consequences (26:00 - 29:00)**
*   Bring it back down to earth. This isn't just about creepy ads.
*   Mention domestic violence survivors, swatting, and scams targeting the elderly using relatives' names. This data physically endangers marginalized people.

**Slide 11 & 12: The Invisible Web & Scale (29:00 - 33:00)**
*   Transition from "Public Records" to "Hidden Trackers".
*   Look at the chart: "Google is on 72% of websites. Even if you use an iPhone, Safari, and duckduckgo... if you visit a news site, Google is watching you."

**Slide 13: The Data Economy (33:00 - 35:00)**
*   Tackle the "Personalized Ads" myth. 
*   "They aren't trying to show you things you like. They are profiling your emotional states to trigger impulse purchases."

**Slide 14: Security Failures (35:00 - 38:00)**
*   "Data is a toxic asset." If a company collects it, eventually it will be stolen.
*   Mention the massive 2024 NPD breach where almost every American's SSN was leaked. "They scraped your data without permission, and then they lost it."

**Slide 15: The Architecture (38:00 - 40:00)**
*   Summarize the problem: It is intentional. Surveillance is the business model of the internet.

**Slide 16: Look For Yourself (40:00 - 41:00)**
*   Challenge the audience: Go home tonight and request your Google or Instagram data. Seeing thousands of pages of your own GPS coordinates is the best wake-up call.

**Slide 17 & 18: Practical Steps & The Tool Belt (41:00 - 46:00)**
*   Time for solutions. **Do not overwhelm them.** 
*   "If you only do one thing today: Install uBlock Origin." Explain that it blocks the trackers we saw on Slide 11.
*   Briefly recommend Firefox, Bitwarden, and Proton. Make it clear that privacy is a spectrum—doing *something* is better than doing *nothing*.

**Slide 19: The Bigger Picture (46:00 - 48:00)**
*   "I shouldn't have to be a cybersecurity expert just to exist online without being tracked."
*   Point out `5calls.org`. Systemic problems require systemic, legislative solutions (a US Privacy Law).

**Slide 20: Closing (48:00 - 50:00)**
*   Bring it back to the book. Who holds the measuring stick?
*   Leave the Appendix/Q&A slide up while you take questions.
